# First 10 Snapshots Playbook

## Purpose

The first 10 snapshots define the quality of the entire system.

Do these carefully.

---

# Goal

Establish:
- structure discipline
- tagging consistency
- artifact extraction habit

---

# What to capture

Start with high-value sessions:

1. System design decisions
2. Important AI conversations
3. Reusable prompts
4. Architecture discussions
5. Writing frameworks
6. Planning sessions
7. Review sessions
8. Problem-solving sessions
9. Strategic thinking
10. Anything you might reuse

---

# What to avoid

Do NOT start with:
- trivial conversations
- low-value chats
- exploratory noise

---

# Each snapshot must include

- clear problem
- key ideas
- explicit decisions
- reusable patterns

---

# Extraction rule

From your first 10 snapshots, you should produce:

- at least 3–5 artifacts
- at least 2–3 concepts

---

# Quality bar

A good snapshot should allow someone else to:

- understand the problem
- follow the reasoning
- reuse the outcome

without needing the original conversation.

---

# Anti-patterns

## Overwriting instead of extending
Keep history. Don’t erase evolution.

## Over-summarizing
Do not lose reasoning.

## Under-structuring
Follow the schema strictly.

---

# Success criteria

After 10 snapshots:

- structure feels natural
- reuse starts happening
- patterns begin to emerge

At that point, Ivy becomes self-reinforcing.

---

# Final note

The first 10 snapshots are not about volume.

They are about setting the standard.
